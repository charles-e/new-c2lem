Monza Murcatto, Captain of the Thousand Swords mercenaries and her brother Beno, are (almost) assassinated by their longtime employer and supposed sponsor, Grand Duke Orzo (with six accomplices).  Monza survives with brutal scars, a ruined right hand and the rest of the book is about her revenge mission to assassinate all seven of her assassins.  Along the way Monza collects her own accomplices: Caul Shivers a warrior from the north who wants to leave behind violence to become "a better man", Cosca, the very charismatic previous captain of of the Thousand Swords, now an alcoholic; Friendly, an institutionalized, counting-obsessed (autistic), simple, powerful, violent man, yet normally docile, Morveer a professional poisoner, amoral, pedantic and self obsessed.  

I love Abercrombie's books because the characters are clearly, vividly legible with clear characteristics and verbal tics as an fantasy RPG player these characters give me ideas to steal about how I can make my 3rd level hobbit rogue a more distinct presence during the game.  And in all of the books these distinct characters are, in addition, amoral, violent, untrustworthy.  To continue the RPG theme, in this book all the characters, the PCs in Monza's crew and the NPCs they overcome (mostly) are horrible people but the PCs have, additionally, sympathetic, plausible back stories such that you easily begin to root for Monza or Caul to win, yet then you encounter a passage where Monza does something completely reprehensible, revolting and you stop: you think to yourself how horrible this is and you think, "JA you fucking tricksie talented bastard making me root for Monza!".
