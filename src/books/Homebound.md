Perhaps it was the part of her that could summon the bitter taste of maror that knew: the telling held the answers, or if not answers, then some kind of ease. She remembered far more than she'd realized: folktales and creation myths and superstitious legends. And in the telling-to Babylon, to herself-she began to feel a new calm.

Meh.  This book didn't know what it was.  There are chunks of it that use the trope of an infocom game like Zork to evoke a far future mission from earth to find a new planet.  But parts of the book, set in the 1980s are about a young lesbian trying to navigate new found romance and the death of her gay brother.  And then the bigger part of it is about a found (jewish) family in a post climate change ocean earth.  But it never made a choice to unify the threads that it started and I got frustrated but by the time I decided I was fed up there were only 10 pages left.
