I think that this books popularity rests on two main pillars: the first pillar is that the protagonist, Neema, is a strong feminist scholar (in *my* brain: "bookstore owner") who has a healthy, happy sex life.  The second is that this is a twisty magical mystery where you can not understand exactly why and what is going on until 90% through the book.  A third pillar is the notion of the raven spirit that, at times, takes over narration and at times takes possession of Neema, and then at times takes the form of a book.
